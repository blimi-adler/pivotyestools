The one thing most owners get wrong

They see an overwhelmed employee and immediately start thinking about who to hire or how to reshuffle the org chart. But the real question isn't who does what – it's why is this broken in the first place.

There are only three reasons a role becomes unworkable: too much volume, wrong tasks, or wrong person for the load. The fix is completely different for each. This tool helps you find out which one you're dealing with.

How it works
1 · Role snapshot – Who is this person and what should they own?
2 · Task audit – Map everything they're actually doing, not just what's on paper.
3 · Diagnose – Is this volume, scope creep, or capacity? Don't skip this step.
4 · Redistribute – Assign each task: stays, moves, drops, or needs a hire.

Step 3 of 4 · Diagnosis

What's actually
broken?

Don't jump to a solution until you've named the problem. The fix is completely different depending on which of these is true.

Root cause – pick the one that fits most

Volume problem – too much, full stop

The tasks that belong here are legitimately more than one person can handle well. The role has grown beyond its original design. They're not doing anything wrong – there's just too much.

Scope creep – wrong tasks in the pile

The volume is manageable, but tasks have accumulated that don't belong here. Other people's work has drifted in. The role has expanded informally without anyone deciding it should.

Capacity problem – the load is right, but they're struggling with it

The tasks are appropriate and the volume is reasonable – but this person isn't keeping up. Could be skill, efficiency, confidence, or fit. The role isn't the problem; the match is.

Mixed – a bit of everything

It's not clean. There's real scope creep AND the load is high AND there are capacity questions. Untangle one at a time – scope first, then volume, then person.
