Step 1 of 6 — Your Best People
Think of 3 people you'd clone if you could.
Past or present employees. Not their skills — how they showed up. What did they have in common?
Why this works: Your instinct about who you'd clone reveals what you actually value — before you've named it.
Step 2 of 6 — The Frustration Flip
What behaviors make you want to pull your hair out?
Enter the behavior that frustrates you — then name the value it reveals on the right.

The flip rule: Every frustration is a value in disguise. Flip the negative into what you'd rather see.
Step 3 of 6 — Your Pride Moments
"This is exactly what we're about."
Think of a moment — with a client, a team, a result — where everything felt right. What was happening? What did someone do that made it feel exactly like you.
What to listen for: The emotion in your answer. What made it feel right tells you more than any list of words.
Step 4 of 6 — What You'd Defend
What would you fire someone for — even a top performer?
Not a skills gap. A character or conduct line they crossed that made the decision easy, even if it was costly.
The real test: A value is only real if you've paid for it. This is where you find your non-negotiables.
Step 5 of 6 — What You Believe
What do you actually believe about people?
Not what you wish. What you've learned. Answer honestly — this reveals the deeper philosophy behind how you lead.
Go deeper: Your beliefs about people shape every hiring, firing, and management decision you make.
Step 6 of 6 — The Team Experience
What do you want your team to feel — not just achieve?
Not the result. The experience of working here. What do you want people to say about what it's like to be on your team?
This is your culture promise. It's what your values feel like from the inside.
