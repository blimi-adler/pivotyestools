The Performance
Evaluation

Before you begin — read this. It takes two minutes and will change how you show up in this conversation.

or keep reading below
When to use this
◷
Annual or scheduled review
A structured, consistent way to evaluate every employee on the same framework. No more winging it, no more vague conversations.
⚠
Performance concern you've been sitting on
Something feels off but you haven't been able to name it. This tool helps you get clear before you say a word to the employee.
💬
Raise or promotion request
An employee asks for more money or a bigger role. Use this to evaluate whether the request is backed by performance — so your answer is defensible, not emotional.
↗
Onboarding milestone check-in (30/60/90 day)
A new hire is wrapping up their ramp period. Use this before the conversation to assess whether they're integrating — or whether something needs to be addressed early.
Why this matters

Most owners wait too long to have performance conversations — and when they finally do, they go in unprepared. The result is a conversation that's either too soft to create change, or too reactive to be fair.

This tool changes that. It forces you to think before you talk — to evaluate the role, the environment, and the person separately before drawing any conclusions.

The goal isn't a score. It's clarity — so you can walk into that room knowing exactly where you stand, what you're saying, and what happens next.

How it works
1
Role setup — Define the role before evaluating the person. Clarity first.
2
Evaluation — Score values, performance, and role-specific responsibilities.
3
GWC — Three yes/no questions that cut through everything else.
4
Pay analysis — Benchmark compensation and evaluate any raise request objectively.
5
Feedback & plan — Write what you'll say, set goals, and export a clean PDF summary.
One ask before you begin
→Answer honestly. Not how you wish things were. How they actually are.
→Separate the person from the role. Some gaps are about the system, not the individual.
→Use this before the meeting — not in it. This is your thinking space, not a live interview tool.
→It takes about 10–15 minutes. Give it the time it deserves.

PivotYesPerformance Evaluation · GWC
Step 3 of 5 · GWC

Three questions.
Yes or no.

All three need to be yes. If any is no — it doesn't mean they're bad. It means they're not in the right seat.

G

Get it

Does this person consistently demonstrate a clear understanding of their role and how it connects to the bigger picture?

W

Want it

Does this person show genuine enthusiasm, motivation, and ownership of their work and the team's success?

C

Capacity for it

Does this person have the skills, experience, and bandwidth to handle their current responsibilities — and grow into more?
